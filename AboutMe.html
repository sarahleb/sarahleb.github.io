About Me

The #Rundown


You may have come to this page seeking answers, or maybe even insight. You won't find all of that here, but it's a good place to start.

As a child, I loved three things more than anything else. The first was reading. As soon as I could read on my own, I was never far from a book. The second was digging through any closed drawer I could find. Much to the chagrin of my family, this included their drawers (and the tops of their closets). I liked to know what people kept. This directly related to the third thing, which was taking things apart and then putting them back together. There was nothing like figuring out exactly how something worked, then making it work again.

College led me to anthropology, where I delved deeper into understanding why people act the way we do. Post-graduation, I worked at a call center, which was illuminating, then at an amazing community bakery. If you ever want to understand people, work in the service industry. Everything becomes clear.

Finally, I decided to throw all my passions together. I pursued archiving because it allows me to work in all the realms I love: I get to dig in to material in order to understand what makes people or institutions do the things they do and put information together in a way that will be meaningful for others.
